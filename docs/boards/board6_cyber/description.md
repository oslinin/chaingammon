# Board 6 — Cyber (The Neural Net Era, late 20th century–present)

## Historical Context

In the late 20th and early 21st centuries, backgammon entered the digital age. The pivotal milestone was **gnubg** (GNU Backgammon) — an open-source neural network evaluation engine that, alongside commercial pioneers Jellyfish and Snowie, dismantled centuries of human dogma. By playing millions of games against itself, the AI proved traditional strategies were often too conservative. It taught players to value flexibility, take calculated early risks, and view the board through pure probability. Today no human consistently beats a top-tier bot, and the world's best players train strictly against neural nets.

**Board aesthetic:** Matte black aluminum, glowing status LEDs, minimalist dark workspace, and terminal screens. Pure black with electric cyan and magenta accent lines, circuit-trace border patterns, and a glowing hex-grid point layout.

## Character: Elena "Zero" Vance

A focused, hyper-analytical grandmaster in her late 20s wearing a sleek dark tech-wear hoodie, seated in a minimalist room lit only by the soft glow of monitors, analyzing positions against a command-line gnubg engine.

**Playing style — The Non-Dogmatic Math Engine.** Elena plays with zero emotion and total disregard for traditional safety. Trained entirely on neural network output, she makes moves that look bafflingly risky to old-school players — leaving slots wide open early to maximize future attacking potential. Every move is optimized to the third decimal point of equity.

## Coin

The coin is a **glowing circuit medallion** — a matte black disc etched with a gold circuit-board trace pattern and a single illuminated LED at its center, representing the neural net nodes that reshaped how humanity plays the oldest game in the world.

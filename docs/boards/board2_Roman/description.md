# Board 2 — Roman (The Tabula Era, 1st century CE)

## Historical Context

The Romans inherited Middle Eastern race games and spread them across the empire. They first played **Ludus Duodecim Scriptorum** ("Game of Twelve Lines") on a three-row board, then streamlined it into **Tabula** ("Table") by the 1st century CE — 24 points, 30 checkers, two or three dice, and rules virtually indistinguishable from modern backgammon. The Emperor Claudius was so obsessed with Tabula that he had a portable board built into his chariot so he could play on the road.

**Board aesthetic:** Marble columns, Roman brass, crimson military banners, and ivory. Cool white and deep crimson with gilded brass borders and laurel-wreath motifs.

## Character: Senator Claudius Marcellus

A sharp-eyed, aristocratic statesman draped in a white toga with a purple border. He plays on a portable brass-bound board resting on a stone table in a bustling Roman villa, effortlessly rolling bone dice while sipping wine.

**Playing style — The Aggressive Gladiator.** Playing under Roman rules that used three dice instead of two, Marcellus is volatile and relentless. He uses the extra movement to launch rapid sweeping attacks, relentlessly hitting exposed enemy checkers. He values momentum over defense and aims to overrun the opponent before they can establish a foothold.

## Coin

The coin bears a Roman brass sestertius — the face of an emperor in profile, laurel-wreathed, struck in gleaming burnished brass, echoing the portable board Claudius carried in his chariot.
